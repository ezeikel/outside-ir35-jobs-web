# Monetisation

Three revenue lines, all legally clean under the start-light trust model, plus
affiliate upside. The guardrail at the bottom is non-negotiable — crossing it
pulls us into the tax chain.

---

## 1. Charge job posters (live at launch)

Paid **native** job postings (featured / standard tiers). Native posters get:

- Richer `JobIR35Signal` options (`SDS_ISSUED`, `CONTRACT_REVIEW_HELD`).
- Once the pool is dense, **access to the verified contractor pool**.

**Aggregated** listings stay **free / source-attributed** to keep inventory
full; paid **native** listings are the differentiated, monetised layer on top.

### Pricing benchmarks (UK, 2025–26)

Most UK boards hide pricing behind sales, but the market ranges (per 28–30 day
post, ex-VAT):

| Board type                                         | Single post             | Bundle (effective/job) | Featured uplift |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- | ---------------------- | --------------- |
| Generalist (Reed, Totaljobs, CV-Library)           | £100–250                | £70–150                | £50–150         |
| Tech/contract niche (CWJobs, Jobserve, Technojobs) | £150–300                | £90–200                | £50–150         |
| Indeed / LinkedIn                                  | PPC (£50–400 effective) | —                      | (higher bid)    |

**Niche, mission-driven boards command a premium** — pocitjobs-style niche
boards sit at the **£200–500/listing** end. Our outside-IR35 niche +
verified-pool access justifies premium pricing **once we have audience**. Early
on: **free/cheap posting for the first 20–50 employers** to seed (the pocitjobs
playbook), then introduce paid tiers.

## 2. Premium contractor subscription (fast-follow, business-expensable)

Position **explicitly as a business tool used to win and deliver contracts** so
it clearly meets the corporation-tax **"wholly and exclusively"** test (the
company-deduction test — _not_ the stricter employee "necessarily" test).

- **Provide a proper VAT invoice** so VAT-registered PSCs can reclaim input VAT.
- Premium unlocks: higher trust-tier features, AI alerts + tailoring, full
  benchmark data, profile prominence.
- Marketing **may note** the expense benefit — but must add **"consult your
  accountant"**. Never state guaranteed tax relief (deductibility depends on the
  contractor's facts).

> This is a genuine wedge: these contractors have limited companies, money to
> spend, and can expense the subscription as a legitimate business cost — far
> lower friction than a consumer subscription.

See [`ir35-trust-model.md`](./ir35-trust-model.md) for the
wholly-and-exclusively grounding.

## 3. Affiliate / partner revenue (later)

IR35 **insurance** (Qdos / Markel / Kingsbridge) and IR35 **contract-review**
providers.

This doubles as the **safest trust signal**: "holds active IR35 /
tax-investigation cover" is defensible where "verified outside IR35" is not.
Revenue + trust in one feature.

---

## Guardrail (non-negotiable)

Keep monetisation to **advertising + SaaS subscription + affiliate**.

**Do NOT** take a cut of placements or handle payments between client and
contractor. That risks reclassification as an **employment business /
fee-payer** — re-entering the Conduct Regs scope and the IR35 / umbrella tax
chain (see trust model, point 10). Our operating model is what keeps us
liability-light; protect it.
